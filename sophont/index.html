Doctor-Facing AI Assistant

Patient's brain MRI shows mild periventricular white matter hyperintensities and recent lab results indicate elevated HbA1c (7.2%). Clinical notes mention increasing fatigue and mild cognitive complaints.

What is the most likely clinical correlation between these findings?

The combination of periventricular white matter hyperintensities on MRI, elevated HbA1c, and cognitive complaints suggests early small vessel disease likely related to poorly controlled diabetes. This pattern is consistent with early diabetic microvascular changes affecting cerebral vessels.
